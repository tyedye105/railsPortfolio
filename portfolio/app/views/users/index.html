A Background Most Breif

Before I began my journey to become a web developer, I worked at my local Fred Meyer. I gained valuable experiences in honing my professional skills in customer service, teamwork, spill response, but by the end I was trapped in soul-curshing cycle of work-eat-sleep. I was ready to pursure something that I was passonaite, something that I could see myself doing for a long time. That something was programming. I had always toyed around with the idea of programming but never took it too seriously, as it seemed like a bunch of black magic I could not even begin to understand. After close to a full year and half of an arduous retail existence, I was ready to try coding.

I enrolled into the Epicodus part-time Introduction to Programming class, to get a feel if becoming a web developer was something I wanted to do. I was only three weeks into that program, and I knew that I was ready to take the deep dive. Months have past since that pivotal moment, and the black magic that was coding, has been de-mystified. Along with my new understanding of programming, I have confidence in my skills using html, css, javascript, and ruby, and as well as my abilities to use jquery and bootstrap to make more a dynamic website, than an unchanging electric brick wall.
